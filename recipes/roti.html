Surinamese Roti

Oyakodon is a classic Japanese rice bowl dish whose name means “parent-and-child donburi,” symbolizing its main ingredients: chicken and egg. Tender chicken pieces and onions are simmered in a savory-sweet broth of dashi, soy sauce, and mirin, then topped with gently cooked beaten eggs for a silky texture. Served over steamed rice, oyakodon is both hearty and comforting, balancing sweet and savory flavors in a simple, homestyle meal that highlights the essence of Japanese comfort food.

Ingredients:

  • 500 grams of potato
  • 300 grams of chicken breast
  • 4 tbsp of masala
  • 250 grams of long beans
  • 1 madame Jeanette pepper
  • Roti wraps

Recipe steps:

  1. Cut the potato, chicken breast, and long beans into bite size pieces
  2. Put the potato in a wok, with a bit of oil and with the chicken
  3. After the chicken is seared shut, add the masala, together with the madame Jeanette pepper and mix it together
  4. Cover the mixture with water, until almost everything is covered, and let it simmer for 15 minutes
  5. After 15 minutes, add the long beans and let it simmer some more for approximately 10 minutes
  6. Heat the roti wraps up in the microwave for 15 seconds each, and put the filling from the wok, together with the roti wraps on a plate to serve
