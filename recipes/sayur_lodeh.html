Sayur Lodeh

Sayur Lodeh” by Choo Yut Shing, CC BY-NC-SA 2.0

Sayur lodeh is a traditional Indonesian vegetable curry, beloved for its rich coconut milk base and fragrant spices. Typically, it features a mix of vegetables such as eggplant, chayote, green beans, cabbage, and tofu, all simmered in a mildly spiced broth flavored with garlic, shallots, galangal, lemongrass, and turmeric. Creamy yet light, sayur lodeh balances sweet, savory, and aromatic notes. It’s often served with rice or lontong (compressed rice cakes), making it a comforting, everyday staple.

Ingredients:

  • 1 small eggplant, cut into chunks
  • 1/2 medium cabbage, roughly chopped
  • 1 chayote (or zucchini), peeled and sliced
  • 100 g green beans, trimmed and cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 medium carrot, sliced
  • 150 g firm tofu or tempeh, cubed and fried (optional)
  • 400 ml coconut milk (1 can)
  • 2 cups vegetable or chicken stock (or water)
  • 2 kaffir lime leaves
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 stalk lemongrass, bruised
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 5 shallots
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 2 red chilies (adjust to spice level)
  • 1 tsp ground turmeric (or 2 cm fresh turmeric)
  • 2 cm galangal

Recipe steps:

  1. Make the spice paste: Blend shallots, garlic, candlenuts, chilies, turmeric, and galangal into a smooth paste.
  2. Sauté: Heat oil in a pot. Fry the spice paste until fragrant and slightly golden. Add lemongrass, bay leaves, and kaffir lime leaves.
  3. Simmer vegetables: Add harder vegetables first (carrot, chayote, green beans). Pour in stock and simmer until half-cooked.
  4. Add coconut milk: Stir in coconut milk and remaining vegetables (cabbage, eggplant). Add tofu or tempeh if using. Simmer gently, avoiding boiling hard, until vegetables are tender.
  5. Season: Adjust taste with salt and a pinch of sugar for balance.
  6. Serve: Enjoy hot with steamed rice or lontong (compressed rice cakes).
